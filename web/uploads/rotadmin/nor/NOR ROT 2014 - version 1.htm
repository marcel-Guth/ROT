37th ROUND TEXEL

JUNE 28, 2014

For Texel rated beach catamarans

NOTICE OF RACE

The 37th Round Texel, the World’s Biggest Catamaran Race, will be sailed around Texel (the Netherlands) on Saturday June 28, 2014. A contingency date is scheduled for Sunday June 29.

The Organizing Authority (OA) is the Ronde om Texel Foundation, in conjunction with the Kustzeilvereniging Westerslag (KZVW), under the authority of the Royal Netherlands Sailing Association (RSNA).

1 RULES

1.1The Round Texel will be governed by the rules as defined in The Racing Rules of Sailing (RRS).

1.2The prescriptions of the Royal Netherlands Sailing Association will apply.

1.3The “National Anti-Doping Regulations for Dutch Sport” will apply.

1.4The Texel Rating Rules will apply.

1.5The sailing instructions may change racing rules.

1.6If there is a conflict between languages, the English text will take precedence.

1.7Every person on board who has his domicile in the Netherlands shall be a member of a Yacht club or other body affiliated to the RNSA and shall have the appropriate license.

2 ADVERTISING AND IDENTIFICATION

2.1Dutch competitors displaying advertising must have an Individual Advertising License issued by the National Authority.

2.2Boats shall be required to display event advertising chosen and supplied by the OA on the 25% front from the bow on the outsides of both hulls while afloat.

2.3While racing, boats shall display start numbers, as provided by the OA. These numbers shall be placed on the mainsail in accordance with the Sailing Instructions.

2.4Competitors may be asked to carry an electronic position indicator / responder beacon on board. This will be furnished by the OA. Selected competitors will be notified.

3 ELIGIBILITY AND ENTRY

3.1The Round Texel is open to all open beach-catamarans with a maximum length of 27 feet and a maximum depth of 0.30 meter (excluding rudder and dagger boards) and with a valid Texel Rating. Any alteration to the design and manufacture of a boat requires a new TR number to be determined for that particular boat (‘one-off’) or boat type as per existing Texel Rating procedures.

3.2Boats without a valid Texel Rating may enter, but can only compete in the ranking for first finishers (‘line honors’). For administrative purposes, the TR will be 50 in this case.

3.3A responsible person of a boat may enter by completing the entry form, available on our website (www.roundtexel.com) . The entry form will be available from March 15 through June 27. A printed entry form is available on request.

3.4In order to appear in a class ranking on boat type, a boat’s valid measurement certificate needs to be shown at the boat’s registration.

3.5Competitors who wish to participate as a Formula 18, must assert their compliance with the Formula 18 class rules, during registration at the Regatta Office. This includes weighing of helm and crew, showing a valid measurement certificate and confirmation of membership of the Formula 18 Class Association.

3.6The minimum age of a person to compete in the Round Texel race is 15 years (as calculated on the day of the Round Texel), i.e. competitors must be born on or before June 28, 1999.

14

SAFETY EQUIPMENT

14.1While afloat each boat must have on board:

a compass;

a paddle per person;

a righting line, at least 8 mm in diameter;

a towing line of at least 10 meters and at least 8 mm in diameter.

14.2While afloat competitors have to wear:

a life jacket;

a wet- or dry suit.

14.3While afloat competitors have to have at least one approved hand flare per person, to be carried on the body.
