ZWITSERLEVEN ROUND TEXEL 2011

For all Texel rated beach catamaran classes

NOTICE OF RACE

 

The 34th Zwitserleven Round Texel, the World’s Biggest Catamaran Race, will be sailed around Texel (the Netherlands) on Saturday June 25 2011 and is part of the Zwitserleven Sailing Week.

 

The Organizing Authority is the Ronde om Texel Foundation, the Royal Netherlands Yachting Union (RNYA/KNWV) in conjunction with the Kustzeilvereniging Westerslag (KZVW).

 

1              RULES

1.1         The Zwitserleven Round Texel will be governed by the rules as defined in The Racing Rules of Sailing (RRS).

1.2         The Texel Rating Rules will apply.

1.3         The sailing instructions may change racing rules.

1.4         If there is a conflict between languages, the English text will take precedence.

 

2              ADVERTISING AND IDENTIFICATION

2.1         Dutch competitors displaying advertising must have an Individual Advertising License issued by the National Authority.

2.2         Boats have to display stickers of this event provided by the Organizing Authority in 2011, on the 25% front from the bow on the insides and on the outsides of both hulls while afloat.

2.3         While racing, boats shall display start numbers. These will be provided by the Organizing Authority. These numbers shall be placed on the mainsail in accordance with the Sailing Instructions issued by the Organizing Authority.

2.4         Competitors may be asked to carry an electronic position indicator / responder beacon on board. This will be furnished by the Organizing Authority. Selected competitors will be notified.

 

3              ELIGIBILITY AND ENTRY

3.1         The Zwitserleven Round Texel is open to all open beach-catamarans with a maximum length of 27 feet and a maximum depth of 0.30 meter (without rudder and dagger boards) and with a valid Texel Rating.

3.2         Competitors will enter in either the Gold Fleet or the Silver Fleet.

3.3         Dutch competitors in the Gold Fleet must have a starting license, issued by the National Authority as mentioned in the prescription of the Royal Netherlands Yachting Union (RNYA) with reference to RRS 78.1 and 78.2 and a boat registration is required as well.

3.4         Competitors not eligible for the Gold Fleet can enter and compete in the Silver Fleet. Competitors in the Silver Fleet will receive a one-day starting license and boat registration from the Organizing Authority.

3.5         The minimum age of a person to enter the Zwitserleven Round Texel race is 15 years (as calculated on the day of the Zwitserleven Round Texel), i.e. competitors must be born on or before June 25 1996.

3.6         Eligible competitors may enter by completing the entry form, available on our website (www.roundtexel.com). The entry form will be available from March 1 through June 24. A printed entry form is available on request.

6              MEASUREMENTS AND RATING SYSTEM

6.1         Spinnakers must have been measured and provided with a stamp or button, either by a National Authority or by the class organization concerned. In the absence of such a stamp or button, measuring will be provided in the main tent on the beach at Paal 17 on:

·         Thursday June 23          10:00 - 12:00

·         Friday June 24              10:00 - 12:00.

6.2         The Texel Rating system will be used for ranking the Overall classification and Open Class classification.

6.3         Dutch competitors with a boat without a Texel Rating have to have their boat measured by the Watersportverbond (KNWV), phone +31(0)30-7513700, Michiel Woort before May 1 2011.

6.4         Foreign competitors with a boat without a Texel Rating have to have their boat measured at home by an official measurer. They then have to register on Thursday June 23 or on Friday June 24 until noon in order to have their Texel Rating established.

10          PENALTY SYSTEM

10.1      For all competitors, rule 44.1 is changed so that the Two-Turns Penalty is replaced by the One-Turn Penalty

10.2      An International Jury will be appointed in accordance with RRS 91(b). Its decisions will be final as provided in RRS 70.5.

 

11          SCORING

11.1      The Zwitserleven Round Texel is a one-race event, so the series consists of one race. No specific point scoring system is applied.

 

12          BERTHING

12.1      Trailers will be assigned places on the beach by the operating tractor drivers of the Organizing Authority. Boats shall be kept in the vicinity of the assigned trailer places.

 

13          RADIO COMMUNICATION

13.1      Except in an emergency, a boat shall neither make radio transmissions while racing nor receive radio communications not available to all boats. This restriction also applies to mobile phones.

 

14          SAFETY EQUIPMENT

14.1      While afloat each boat must have on board:

·        a compass;

·        a paddle per person;

·        a righting line, at least 8 mm in diameter;

·        a towing line of approximately 10 meters and at least 6 mm in diameter.

14.2      While afloat competitors have to wear:

·        a life jacket;

·        a wet- or dry suit.

14.3      While afloat competitors have to have at least one approved hand flare per person, to be carried on the body.

18.1      Each competing boat has to have a valid third party liability insurance policy, racing at the North Sea included and with a minimum coverage of € 1.000.000 per incident.

18.2      In case a boat has caused damage to another boat and has been disqualified, relevant info of that boat’s insurance policy will be made available to the other boat.
