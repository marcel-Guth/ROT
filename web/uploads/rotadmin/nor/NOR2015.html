38th ROUND TEXEL
JUNE 06, 2015
For Texel rated beach catamarans
NOTICE OF RACE
The 38th Round Texel, the World’s Biggest Catamaran Race, will be sailed around
Texel (the Netherlands) on Saturday June 06, 2015. A contingency date is scheduled
for Sunday June 07, 2015.
The Organizing Authority (OA) is the Ronde om Texel Foundation, in conjunction with
the Kustzeilvereniging Westerslag (KZVW), under the authority of the Royal
Netherlands Watersport Association (RNWA).
1
RULES
1.1
The Round Texel will be governed by the rules as defined in The Racing Rules of
Sailing (RRS).
1.2
The Texel Rating Rules will apply.
1.3
The sailing instructions may change racing rules.
1.4
If there is a conflict between languages, the English text will take precedence.
1.5
Every person on board who has his domicile in the Netherlands shall have the
appropriate license.
2
ADVERTISING AND IDENTIFICATION
2.1
Boats shall be required to display event advertising chosen and supplied by the OA
on the 25% front from the bow on the outsides of both hulls while afloat.
2.2
While racing, boats shall display start numbers, as provided by the OA. These
numbers shall be placed on the mainsail in accordance with the Sailing Instructions.
2.3
Competitors may be asked to carry an electronic position indicator / responder
beacon on board. This will be furnished by the OA. Selected competitors will be
notified.
3
ELIGIBILITY AND ENTRY
3.1
The Round Texel is open to all open beach-catamarans with a maximum length of
27 feet and a maximum depth of 0.30 meter (excluding rudder and dagger boards)
and with a valid Texel Rating. Any alteration to the design and manufacture of a
boat requires a new TR number to be determined for that particular boat (‘one-off’)
or boat type as per existing Texel Rating procedures.
3.2
Boats without a valid Texel Rating may enter, but can only compete in the ranking
for first finishers (‘line honors’). For administrative purposes, the TR will be 50 in
this case.
3.3
A responsible person of a boat may enter by completing the entry form, available on
our website (www.roundtexel.com ) . The entry form will be available from March 23
through June 05. A printed entry form is available on request.
3.4
In order to appear in a class ranking on boat type, a boat’s valid measurement
certificate needs to be shown at the boat’s registration.
3.5
Competitors who wish to participate as a Formula 18, must assert their compliance
with the Formula 18 class rules, during registration at the Regatta Office. This
includes weighing of helm and crew, showing a valid measurement certificate and
confirmation of membership of the Formula 18 Class Association. Entry should also
be made as boat type “Formula 18”, instead of manufacturer name and/or -model.
3.6
The minimum age of a person to compete in the Round Texel race is 15 years (as
calculated on the day of the Round Texel 2015), i.e. competitors must be born on or
before June 06, 2000.

• Friday June 05
10:00 - 12:00

6.2
The Texel Rating system will be used for ranking the Overall classification and Open
Class classification.
6.3
Dutch competitors with a boat without a Texel Rating have to have their boat
measured by the Royal Netherlands Watersport Association (‘Watersportverbond’),
phone +31(0)30-7513700, Michiel Woort before May 06, 2015.

PENALTY SYSTEM
10.1
For all competitors, rule 44 is changed so that the Two-Turns Penalty is replaced by
the One-Turn Penalty.
10.2
An international jury will be appointed. Decisions of the international jury will be
final as provided in rule 70.5.
11
SCORING
11.1
The Round Texel is a one-race event, so the series consists of one race.
12
BERTHING
12.1
Trailers will be assigned places on the beach by the operating tractor drivers of the
Organizing Authority. Boats shall be kept in the vicinity of the assigned trailer
places.
13
RADIO COMMUNICATION
13.1
Except in an emergency, a boat shall neither make radio transmissions while racing
nor receive radio communications not available to all boats. This restriction also
applies to mobile phones.
14
SAFETY EQUIPMENT
14.1
While afloat each boat must have on board:
• a compass;
• a paddle per person;
• a righting line, at least 8 mm in diameter;
• a towing line of at least 10 meters and at least 8 mm in diameter.

14.2

18
INSURANCE
18.1
Each competing boat has to have a valid third party liability insurance policy, racing
at the North Sea included and with a minimum coverage of € 1.500.000 per
incident.
18.2
In case a boat has caused damage to another boat and has been disqualified,
relevant info of that boat’s insurance policy will be made available to the other boat.
